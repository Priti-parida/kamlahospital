Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopy, also known as keyhole surgery, is a minimally invasive procedure performed in the abdominal (belly) area. Compared to traditional surgery, laparoscopy offers several advantages, such as faster recovery, reduced pain, and smaller scars.

This procedure requires specialized training, and patients who undergo keyhole surgery typically experience a quicker recovery.

Laparoscopy is commonly used to examine or operate on the abdominal or pelvic cavities. It is conducted under general anesthesia, usually by a surgeon or gynecologist.

Uses of Laparoscopic Surgery

  • Assess painful or heavy periods
  • Remove the uterus, fallopian tubes, or ovaries
  • Diagnose or remove ovarian cysts
  • Assess female infertility
  • Treat ectopic pregnancy
  • Remove cysts or other tumors
  • Remove the gallbladder
  • Remove parts of the intestine
  • Take biopsies for testing
  • Investigate chronic abdominal or pelvic pain

Post-procedure Recovery

After the procedure, you may feel some soreness around the incision sites and experience shoulder pain due to the gas used to inflate your abdomen. After a few hours in the recovery room, you are likely to be discharged with specific care instructions.

Possible Complications

  • Bleeding from the incisions
  • Infection
  • Damage to an organ or blood vessel
  • Small risk of anesthesia-related complications

When to Seek Medical Advice

  • Persistent or worsening pain
  • Increasing shoulder pain
  • Urinary issues
  • Persistent bleeding
  • Fever or high temperature
  • Delayed recovery

Specific Laparoscopic Procedures

Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy:

Gallbladder removal is a common laparoscopic surgery, allowing for faster recovery.

Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication:

Used for treating GERD, this procedure improves esophagus function.

Laparoscopic Ventral Hernia Repair:

Hernias are repaired using mesh for a more durable outcome with reduced postoperative pain.

Laparoscopic Inguinal Hernia Repair:

Most inguinal hernias are repaired using a polypropylene mesh for a durable outcome.

Laparoscopic Colon Resection:

Parts or the entire colon can be removed with laparoscopic techniques for faster recovery and shorter hospital stays.
